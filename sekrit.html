what are those numbers you used sometimes?

a while ago i got a bit obsessed about the bibi-binary system of numbers.
here is a wikipedia article discussing the digits (or nybbles, as i preferred) that it uses, alongside their hindu-arabic equivalents in binary, decimal and hex(adecimal). i'd usually attach their decimal counterparts if you ever got confused.

that's cool and all, but why don't they show up on my font?

simply put, unicode doesn't support them.
the only font i'm aware of that supports them, catrinity, maps them to private use areas that display as boxes or other "placeholder" characters in other fonts.

why don't you use them anymore?

hex(adecimal) and most other bases aren't the best way to count.
